About me

Natalia Madrid is a Ph.D. student in Economics at the University of California San Diego and a current Fulbright Scholar. Her research lies at the intersection of macroeconomics, finance, and environmental economics, with a focus on climate finance, carbon pricing, and the energy transition. She studies how climate risks and climate policy affect financial markets, investment decisions, and economic outcomes.

Prior to beginning her Ph.D., Natalia earned a Bachelor’s degree in Engineering Sciences and a Master’s degree in Financial Management. She has received several scholarships and recognitions for academic excellence, including the Fulbright Scholarship, the Outstanding Woman Engineer Award from the University of Concepción, and the Bicentennial Scholarship awarded by the Chilean Ministry of Education.
