Research

My research agenda is driven by three central questions:

  • How do we finance the green transition?
  • How do we mitigate and adapt to the impacts of climate change?
  • How do we reduce inequalities driven by climate change?

My work lies at the intersection of environmental economics and macro-finance, focusing on how policy mechanisms influence firm behavior, investment decisions, and the transition to a low-carbon economy.

My current project explores the interaction between carbon pricing mechanisms and firm-level investment behavior, particularly in green technologies. I examine whether carbon pricing policies incentivize or deter green investment, and how these effects are shaped by broader macroeconomic conditions. Through this analysis, I aim to shed light on the effectiveness of policy tools in promoting sustainable investment and mitigating the effects of climate change.
